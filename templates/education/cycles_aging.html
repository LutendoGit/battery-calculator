‚ö†Ô∏è Degradation Mechanisms

Batteries age through several physical and chemical processes:

1. SEI Layer Growth

The Solid Electrolyte Interphase layer thickens over time, increasing internal resistance.

2. Electrolyte Decomposition

The electrolyte breaks down, reducing ion conductivity and increasing resistance.

3. Active Material Loss

Cathode and anode particles dissolve or become disconnected, reducing capacity.

4. Electrode Cracking

Repeated expansion and contraction causes physical cracks in electrode materials.

Temperature Management (Most Important!)

  • Keep batteries cool: 15-25¬∞C is ideal
  • Avoid heat: Every 10¬∞C increase doubles aging rate
  • Never store in hot cars or direct sunlight

Charging Habits

  • Avoid 100% charge: Keep below 90% for daily use
  • Avoid 0% discharge: Recharge before 10% remaining
  • Ideal range: Keep between 20-80% SOC

Usage Patterns

  • Use consistent C-rates: Avoid extreme currents
  • Shallow discharges are gentler than deep ones
  • Regular use is better than long storage

Calendar Storage

  • Store at 40-60% SOC (not 0% or 100%)
  • Cool location (below 25¬∞C)
  • Dry environment
  • Check charge level every 3-6 months
